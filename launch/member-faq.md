# Member FAQ — Draft

For the SEA website or a help page. Keep it short and friendly.

---

## How do I access Existential Analysis online?

Your SEA membership includes online access to the journal. You'll receive an email with a link to set your password on the journal site. Once that's done, just log in at [OJS login URL] and you're in.

## I didn't receive a setup email

Check your spam or junk folder first. If it's not there, contact us at [support email] and we'll resend it.

## The link in my email has expired

No problem — go to [OJS login URL], click "Forgot your password?", and enter your email address. You'll get a new link straight away.

## I already have an account on the journal site

If it's under the same email as your SEA membership, you're all set — your membership access will be linked automatically.

If it's under a different email, the easiest fix is to update your email on the membership site to match, or get in touch and we'll sort it out.

## I'm logged in but it's asking me to pay

This usually means the sync between your membership and the journal hasn't completed. Contact us at [support email] with your name and email, and we'll fix it quickly.

## Can I use a different email for the journal?

Your journal access is linked to your SEA membership email. If you'd like to change it, update your email on the membership site at [WP profile URL] and it'll carry across to the journal automatically.

## I've cancelled my membership — can I still read articles?

Your journal access continues until your current membership period ends. After that, you can still purchase individual articles or issues directly from the journal site.
